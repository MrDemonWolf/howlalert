# Privacy Policy

**Last updated: April 9, 2026**

MrDemonWolf, Inc. ("we", "us", or "our") operates the HowlAlert application (the "Service"). This policy explains what data HowlAlert collects, why, and your rights under the GDPR (EU) 2016/679 and applicable privacy laws.

## 1. Who we are (Controller)

**Data Controller:** MrDemonWolf, Inc.
**Contact:** privacy@howlalert.com

## 2. What data we collect

HowlAlert collects the minimum data necessary to deliver push notifications.

| Data | Description | Personal data? |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| **APNs device token** | A pseudonymous identifier issued by Apple that allows us to send notifications to your specific device | Yes — device identifier (Art. 4(1) GDPR; Recital 30) |
| **Pairing secret** | A short random string used to authenticate your device pairing | Not personal data on its own |
| **Usage metrics** | Claude Code token usage percentage, pace status, session token count | Not personal data — no link to your identity |

**We do not collect:** your name, email address, IP address, location, health data, or any other personal information. HowlAlert has no user accounts.

## 3. Why we process your data (purpose and lawful basis)

| Purpose | Lawful basis |
|---------|-------------|
| Relaying your APNs device token to Apple to deliver push notifications when your Claude Code usage approaches limits | **Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR** — processing necessary for the performance of the contract (you installed HowlAlert specifically to receive these notifications) |

## 4. How your data flows

When you trigger a threshold alert:

1. The HowlAlert macOS app sends your **device token** and usage metrics to our Cloudflare Worker (`howlalert-worker.mrdemonwolf.workers.dev`)
2. The Worker signs an Apple Push Notification JWT and forwards the payload to **Apple APNs** (`api.push.apple.com`)
3. Apple delivers the notification to your device
4. **Nothing is stored.** The Worker is stateless — no database, no KV store, no logs.

Your device token is also synced between your macOS and iOS apps via **Apple CloudKit** (private database, iCloud infrastructure). We never see or store this token ourselves — it lives only in Apple's CloudKit containers.

## 5. Our processors

| Processor | Role | Location | Safeguards |
|-----------|------|----------|-----------|
| **Cloudflare, Inc.** | Push relay (Workers edge infrastructure) | EU / US | [Cloudflare DPA](https://www.cloudflare.com/cloudflare-customer-dpa/); SCCs for EU→US transfers |
| **Apple Inc.** | APNs push delivery; CloudKit device-token sync | US | Apple Developer DPA; EU–US Data Privacy Framework |

## 6. International transfers

Your device token is transferred to Apple's APNs servers in the United States. Apple participates in the EU–US Data Privacy Framework and provides Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) as an additional transfer mechanism. Cloudflare's DPA includes SCCs for EU–US data transfers.

## 7. Data retention

**We retain nothing.** The Cloudflare Worker processes each request and discards all data immediately. Cloudflare's own infrastructure access logs (if any) are subject to [Cloudflare's privacy policy](https://www.cloudflare.com/privacypolicy/) and are not under our control.

Apple's CloudKit retains your device token until you unpair your device or delete the app.

## 8. Your rights (GDPR Art. 15–22)

You have the right to:

- **Access** the personal data we hold about you (Art. 15)
- **Rectification** of inaccurate data (Art. 16)
- **Erasure** ("right to be forgotten") (Art. 17) — because we store nothing, uninstalling HowlAlert and revoking CloudKit access achieves this immediately
- **Portability** of your data (Art. 20)
- **Object** to processing (Art. 21)
- **Lodge a complaint** with your supervisory authority (Art. 77)

To exercise any right or to contact our DPO: **privacy@howlalert.com**

## 9. Children

HowlAlert is not directed at children under 16. We do not knowingly collect data from children.

## 10. Changes to this policy

We will notify users of material changes via an in-app notice or update to this page. The "Last updated" date above will always reflect the current version.
